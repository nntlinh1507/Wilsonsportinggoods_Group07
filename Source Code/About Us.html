    Wilson Sporting Goods' business is balanced through its diverse sporting and product portfolio as well as presence in all major markets. The offering of sports equipment, cycling apparel, footwear and accessories covers a wide range of sports, including tennis, badminton, golf, American football, soccer, baseball, basketball, alpine skiing, snowboarding, cross-country skiing, running, hiking, and diving.

    Wilson Sporting Goods sells its products directly to consumers through brand stores, factory stores and e-commerce, and through commercial customers in sporting goods chains, retailers, and retailers. specialty, reseller, fitness club and distributor. In December 2020, Wilson Sporting Goods' own sales organization covered 28 countries.

    Wilson Sporting Goods' strategy emphasizes excellence in creating consumer-centric products. Through continuous research and development, Wilson Sporting Goods seeks to develop new and better sporting goods to appeal to its consumers and commercial customers. Products are designed to improve athletes' performance, help them achieve their goals, and give them more enjoyment in the activity of their choice.

    The Wilson Sports Goods Company is an American sports equipment manufacturer based in Chicago, Illinois. Since 1989, it has been a subsidiary then Finnish group Amer Sports, since 2019 itself a subsidiary of the Chinese group ANTA Sports.[3] Wilson makes equipment for a variety of sports, among them baseball, badminton, American football, basketball, fastpitch softball, golf, racquets, soccer, squash, tennis, pickles, and volleyball .

    The company owns the brands Atec, DeMarini, EvoShield, Louisville Slugger, and Luxilon to provide sports equipment and protective gear for baseball, baseball, softball, and tennis.

    In 2020, total net sales of Wilson Sporting Goods reached EUR 2,206.4 million (continued). At the end of the year, the Group employed 8,887 people.
